What are the key properties that work on flex containers and flex items?

In Flexbox, certain properties are designed specifically for flex containers, while others apply to flex items within those containers. For flex containers, three key properties are `display`, `justify-content`, and `align-items`. The `display` property must be set to `flex` or `inline-flex` to activate Flexbox behavior. `Justify-content` controls the alignment of items along the main axis, allowing you to space them evenly or align them to the start, center, or end. `Align-items` aligns items along the cross axis, determining how flex items are stretched or centered vertically (or horizontally, depending on flex direction). For flex items, useful properties include `order`, `flex-grow`, and `align-self`. `Order` lets you rearrange the visual order of items regardless of their HTML order. `Flex-grow` defines how much a flex item should grow relative to the others when extra space is available. `Align-self` allows an individual item to override the containerâ€™s `align-items` setting and align itself independently along the cross axis.
